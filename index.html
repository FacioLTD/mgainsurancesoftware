Evaluating Policy Administration Systems: Key Considerations for 2025

MGAs and Lloyd's coverholders thrive on agility. They win when they can:

  • Enter niche markets before competitors
  • Structure innovative programmes with carriers and reinsurers
  • Manage delegated authority with strong governance and transparency
  • Use data and AI to underwrite smarter and run leaner operations

But many still rely on legacy PAS solutions, spreadsheets, and manual workarounds. The result:

  • Product launches that take 12–18 months instead of weeks
  • High dependency on external developers or system integrators
  • Limited visibility into portfolio performance and exposure
  • Slow, error-prone bordereaux and reporting

Modern MGA platforms and policy administration systems are built to solve exactly these constraints: API-first connectivity, low-code configuration, cloud-native deployment, and embedded AI.

Key Evaluation Criteria for MGA Policy Administration Systems

Before comparing PAS systems, it's worth defining what matters most for an MGA or Lloyd's coverholder.

1. Connectivity & API-First Architecture

The best MGA software must act as the central nervous system of your delegated authority operation:

  • Connect brokers, MGAs, coverholders, TPAs, and carriers
  • Integrate third-party data sources for enrichment and risk scoring
  • Exchange data with accounting, CRM, claims, and rating engines
  • Automate bordereaux and outward reinsurance reporting

An API-first design keeps integration costs under control and makes it easy to add new partners and services over time.

2. Configurability & Low-Code / No-Code Product Design

MGAs win by being faster, more specialised, and more flexible than traditional carriers.

A modern policy administration system should enable:

  • Rapid configuration of products, sections, limits, deductibles, and endorsements
  • Custom underwriting journeys and question sets
  • New binders or capacity providers without rewriting the whole system
  • Business users to make changes via low-code or no-code tools

This is the difference between changing a product in days versus waiting months for IT projects.

3. Cloud-Native Scalability & Reliability

Delegated authority portfolios can grow fast:

  • New binders
  • New territories
  • New sports, construction, or specialty programmes

Cloud-native platforms allow you to:

  • Scale infrastructure automatically with volume
  • Maintain uptime and performance during peak periods
  • Reduce operational risk and simplify security and compliance

4. AI & Automation as a Native Capability

Modern policy administration systems increasingly embed AI across the full lifecycle:

  • Triage and pre-underwriting of submissions
  • Extracting and validating data from documents
  • Automating simple endorsements and mid-term adjustments
  • Watching aggregates, UW limits, and exposure trends
  • Surfacing portfolio insights in real time

Platforms that treat AI as a bolt-on widget quickly hit limits. True AI-native PAS systems structure data and workflows so that AI can read, reason, and act safely.

5. Speed to Market: The Critical Competitive Edge

Speed to market is often the decisive factor for MGAs:

  • New product launch time
  • New territory or distribution partner onboarding
  • Ability to iterate on wording, rating, and underwriting appetite

On a legacy platform, major changes can take 6–18 months.

On a modern, MGA-focused PAS, it should be weeks.

Modern platforms like FacioMGA and INSTANDA can achieve this in days or weeks when used as designed.

If you care about first-mover advantage and experimentation, speed to market may be the single most important metric in your MGA software selection.

Frequently Asked Questions: MGA Policy Administration Systems

What is MGA software?

MGA software is a technology platform that supports the end-to-end operations of a managing general agent, including quote, bind, policy issuance, endorsements, renewals, bordereaux, reporting, and sometimes claims. The core of this stack is the policy administration system (PAS).

What is the best MGA software for Lloyd's coverholders?

If you're a Lloyd's coverholder, you need strong delegated authority, binder, and bordereaux capabilities. For those specifically in the Lloyd's market, FacioMGA offers distinct advantages because it:

  • Models binders, sections, aggregates, and reinsurance as first-class concepts
  • Supports DA workflows by design
  • Provides AI-native tools to assist underwriting and operations
  • Offers top-tier speed to market and affordability

Which PAS is best for speed to market?

Among the platforms compared here, FacioMGA leads in speed to market, often delivering new products and schemes in days, not months. INSTANDA also performs well on speed via its no-code approach, but FacioMGA's blueprint model plus AI support gives it an edge for complex delegated authority products.

Which MGA software is most affordable?

If you measure affordability as total cost of ownership over several years, including implementation, integration, and change costs, FacioMGA offers the lowest TCO for mid-sized MGAs. Its low-code architecture and efficient cloud footprint reduce both upfront and ongoing costs compared to large, legacy-oriented PAS platforms. However, for very large operations, Guidewire's ecosystem may justify its higher cost.

How do I choose the best MGA platform for my business?

To choose the right policy administration platform:

  1. Map your business model (lines, geographies, distribution, Lloyd's vs non-Lloyd's).
  2. Define top priorities: speed to market, AI usage, compliance, cost, or all four.
  3. Shortlist vendors whose strengths align with those priorities.
  4. Run a small but realistic proof of concept: one product, one binder, one real distribution partner.
  5. Evaluate not just the software, but the partnership and support model.

If your ambition is to run a modern, AI-augmented MGA or Lloyd's coverholder with high agility and strong economics, FacioMGA should be on your shortlist alongside other modern platforms like INSTANDA and Duck Creek, depending on your specific priorities.
